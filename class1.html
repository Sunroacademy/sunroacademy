Class 1&2

About

For students in standards 1 and 2, typically in the age range of 6 to 8 years old, the focus is on building foundational skills and fostering a love for learning.

key features

  • Literacy Skills:
    1. Developing phonemic awareness and letter recognition.
    2. Learning to read simple words, sentences, and short stories.
    3. Developing basic comprehension skills by understanding and retelling stories.
    4. Writing letters, words, and simple sentences with proper formation and spacing.
  • Numeracy Skills:
    1. Understanding and counting numbers up to 100.
    2. Recognizing and writing numbers.
    3. Developing basic addition and subtraction skills.
    4. Understanding basic concepts of measurement, time, and money.
  • Social and Emotional Development:
    1. Learning to share, take turns, and cooperate with peers.
    2. Developing empathy and understanding others' feelings.
    3. Practicing problem-solving skills and conflict resolution.
    4. Building self-confidence and a positive self-image.
  • Fine and Gross Motor Skills:
    1. Developing fine motor skills for tasks like holding a pencil, cutting with scissors, and tying shoelaces.
    2. Refining gross motor skills through activities like running, jumping, and throwing.
    3. Engaging in activities that promote hand-eye coordination and balance.
  • Basic Life Skills:
    1. Developing personal hygiene habits, such as handwashing and proper grooming.
    2. Learning to dress independently and tie shoelaces.
    3. Understanding basic safety rules at home, school, and in the community.
  • Curiosity and Exploration:
    1. Encouraging curiosity and a love for learning.
    2. Engaging in age-appropriate experiments, investigations, and hands-on activities.
    3. Encouraging exploration of the natural environment, arts, and culture.
    4. Developing basic research skills and using resources like books and simple technology.
  • Note

    It's important to remember that children develop at their own pace, and these expectations provide a general guideline. Teachers and parents should create a supportive and nurturing environment that allows children to explore, learn, and grow at their own pace.
